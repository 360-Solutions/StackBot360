The Batch Learning Engine

Most flashcard apps make you type out cards one by one. StackBot360 lets you paste massive lists of data directly into the Editor. A stack is basically a Text list. Whether it is an AI-generated list of Spanish vocabulary, OCR text from a science textbook, or a lecture glossary, if it is separated by a line break, the Bot will read it as separate flashcards.

You can easily ask your favorite AI to format a list of almost anything you are studying. Or create a simple line by line list yourself in any text editor. You can also easily share and import lists from your fellow students, coworkers, etc, because they are basically just a simple text list. The program will designate the first two lines of the stack to be reserved as the title of the stack and the date and timestamp of the stacks last save.

The "Sleep & Jog" Hack

By default, the program will read these flashcards to you with the internal text to voice of your device. Because StackBot360 waits for the system text-to-speech voice to finish reading before moving to the next card, it creates a hands-free learning loop. You can adjust the tempo, press play, and let the Bot drill information into your ears while you jog, commute, or go to sleep.

Note: To improve the robot voice quality, go to your iOS Settings > Accessibility > Spoken Content > Voices and download a premium voice package.
